Novak Djokovic-Autobiography

Novak Djokovic ( Belgrade , May 22, 1987 ) is a Serbian professional tennis player . He spent 373 weeks at the top of the ATP list, which is the longest period in the history of the open era. Many tennis critics, former players and teammates consider Djokovic one of the best tennis players in history. [a] He has been playing professionally since 2003 . years. In his career so far, he has won 21 titles at Grand Slam tournaments: nine times the Australian Open , seven times Wimbledon , three times the US Open and two times Roland Garros. He is one of the five tennis players in the Open era who won the Grand Slam career . By winning Roland Garros in 2016, he became the first tennis player, after Rod Laver in 1969, to win all four Grand Slam tournaments in a row and the only one to do so on three different surfaces. He is the first tennis player in the Open era to win every Grand Slam tournament at least twice. He holds the record for most trophies won at the Australian Open and is the only one to have triumphed three times and twice in a row in the Open era. He holds the record with seven completed years as the number one tennis player in the world and half of the earnings from the men's singles tournament.
He is the winner of six Masters Cup titles and the only one who has triumphed four times in a row. Djokovic is the first and only player to win all nine tournaments from the ATP Masters 1000 series, the only one to win all the Masters 1000 at least twice. In total, he won thirty-eight tournaments of this category and holds the record, and has six won in one season, which is recorder. He won a bronze medal at the 2008 Olympic Games in Beijing . Together with his teammates from the Serbian national team, he won the Davis Cup title in 2010 and the ATP Cup in 2020 .
With Rafael Nadal , he achieved one of the greatest rivalries in the history of tennis. They played a record number of duels in the Open era, a record eighteen duels in Grand Slam tournaments and the longest Grand Slam final in Melbourne in 2012 . Djokovic's rivalry with the Swiss Roger Federer , against whom he played fifty times, was noted.
In addition to being declared the best athlete and tennis player in Serbia and the world, Djokovic was awarded the Order of the Star of Karađorđe , the Order of Saint Sava from the Serbian Orthodox Church and the Order of the Republic of Srpska . Also, Novak is the creator of the "Novak Đoković" Foundation, which is actively engaged in providing assistance to children in socially disadvantaged areas, as well as other humanitarian actions.

Tennis career

As a junior Djokovic was a three-time European champion in 2001, in singles, doubles and team competition. [13] The following season, he became the best in Europe in the competition of tennis players under the age of 16. [14] After five ITF tournaments, Djokovic was ranked 40th among the best juniors in the world. As a junior, he had a ratio of 40 wins and 11 losses in singles competition, or 23 wins and 6 losses in doubles competition. As a member of the Yugoslav national team in 2001, he reached the final of the junior Davis Cup for tennis players under 14 years of age, in which he lost his match in the singles competition.
